1
Find a Village

The first thing you need is a villager. The easiest way is to find a naturally generated village. Villages spawn in these biomes:

  • Plains (most common)
  • Desert
  • Savanna
  • Taiga
  • Snowy Plains

Explore during the day and look for clusters of buildings on relatively flat terrain. You can also use the /locate village command if cheats are enabled.

No village nearby? You can also find villagers by curing Zombie Villagers in the wild — covered in Step 9.
2
Find an Unemployed Villager

You need a villager that doesn't already have a job. Look for one wearing a plain brown robe with no distinctive features. These are unemployed and ready to take a profession.

Watch out for Nitwits! Villagers wearing green robes are Nitwits — they can never take a job. Don't waste your Brewing Stand on them.

If all villagers in the village are employed, you can either:

  • Break another villager's job block (they'll become unemployed if you haven't traded with them)
  • Breed two villagers by giving them food and providing extra beds
3
Get a Blaze Rod

To craft a Brewing Stand, you need a Blaze Rod. This means you'll need to visit the Nether.

  • Build a Nether Portal (10 Obsidian minimum) and light it with Flint & Steel
  • Find a Nether Fortress — a large dark brick structure
  • Locate a Blaze Spawner inside the fortress
  • Kill Blazes until you get at least 1 Blaze Rod (they drop 0-1 each)
Bring gear! The Nether is dangerous. Take at least iron armor, a shield, and fire resistance potions if you have them. Blazes shoot fireballs.
4
Craft the Brewing Stand

Back in the Overworld, craft a Brewing Stand at a crafting table:

1 Blaze Rod
+ 3 Cobblestone (or Blackstone)

Place the Blaze Rod in the top-center slot and the three Cobblestone across the bottom row.

Already found one? Villages with a Cleric already in them contain a Brewing Stand in the church building. You can take it and reuse it.
5
Place the Brewing Stand Near the Villager

Place the Brewing Stand on the ground within reach of your unemployed villager. The villager needs to be able to pathfind to it.

  • Place it within about 48 blocks of the villager (closer is better)
  • Make sure there's a clear path — no walls, fences, or gaps blocking them
  • The villager must have line-of-sight access to walk up and claim the block
Other villagers can steal it! If there are other unemployed villagers nearby, one of them might claim the Brewing Stand first. Isolate your target villager to avoid this.
6
Wait for the Transformation

The villager will walk to the Brewing Stand and claim it as their job block. You'll see green sparkle particles and their outfit will change to purple Cleric robes.

Purple robes = success

This only happens during the villager's work hours (approximately 2000-9000 in-game ticks, or daytime). If it's night, they'll sleep first and claim it in the morning.

Don't like the trades? If the Cleric's Novice trades aren't what you want, break the Brewing Stand and place it again. They'll unclaim it and reclaim with new random trades. Repeat until you're happy.
7
Lock in Their Trades

Once you're satisfied with the Cleric's Novice-level offers, make at least one trade with them. Right-click the Cleric to open the trading UI.

The easiest first trade is usually:

32 Rotten Flesh
→
1 Emerald
This is permanent! After your first trade, this villager's profession is locked forever. They can never become a different profession, even if you break their Brewing Stand.
8

The big unlocks:

  • Expert — Ender Pearls (5 Emeralds each)
  • Master — Bottles o' Enchanting (3 Emeralds each)
Fastest leveling: Buy and sell everything available at each level. The Rotten Flesh trade alone can power you through Novice quickly. Sell Gold Ingots at Apprentice, then buy Glowstone at Journeyman.
9
Get Maximum Discounts (Zombie Curing)

This is the advanced strategy that turns a good Cleric into an incredible one. Curing a Zombie Villager gives permanent trade discounts.

How to cure a Zombie Villager:

  • Find or create a Zombie Villager (zombies have a 5% chance to spawn as one, or a zombie can convert a villager on Hard difficulty)
  • Throw a Splash Potion of Weakness at them
  • Right-click them with a Golden Apple
  • Wait 3-5 minutes — they'll shake and turn back into a normal villager
Stack the discounts! On Java Edition, you can zombify and re-cure the same villager multiple times. Each cure stacks the discount further. After 5 cures, Ender Pearls can cost just 1 Emerald and XP bottles drop to 1 Emerald.
Risk: Villagers have a chance to die when zombified on Normal difficulty (50% death rate). On Hard difficulty they always convert. Only attempt this on Hard mode to guarantee survival.
10
Build a Trading Hall

Now scale up. One Cleric is good — a hall full of them is an empire. Here's how to set one up:

  • Build a row of small cells (1x1 or 2x1), each with a bed and a Brewing Stand
  • Place one villager in each cell — use minecarts or boats to transport them
  • Leave a walkway in front so you can access their trade UI
  • Add lighting (torches, lanterns, Glowstone) to prevent hostile mob spawns
  • Use fences or glass to keep them secure

Pair it with these farms for an unstoppable economy:

Zombie Farm
Nether Wart Farm
Gold Farm
You're done! With a full trading hall of cured Clerics, you'll have unlimited Emeralds, Ender Pearls, XP Bottles, and Glowstone at your fingertips.
